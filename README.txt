1)Project Overview
Analyzed Credit Card Spending Habits in India using a dataset of thousands of credit card transactions.Data categorized by city, date, card type, expense type, gender, and transaction amount.
2)Objective: Uncover regional spending patterns, temporal trends, card type influences, expense variations, and gender-based differences in spending.
Utilized data-driven insights and visualizations to provide a comprehensive understanding of consumer behavior and trends in the Indian market.
Learning and Analysis
3)Identified significant regional spending patterns across various Indian cities.
4)Determined the impact of different card types on consumer spending behavior.
5)Explored gender-based differences in spending habits.
6)Analyzed temporal trends to reveal seasonal variations and peak spending periods.
7)Categorized expenses to highlight consumer priorities and financial behaviors.
8)Demonstrated the critical role of data in understanding consumer trends and in tailoring financial products to diverse consumer needs.
